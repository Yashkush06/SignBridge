CRITICAL DESIGN INSTRUCTION

The application must NOT look AI-generated.

Most AI-generated interfaces have obvious characteristics:
- Generic SaaS layouts
- Huge rounded corners everywhere
- Random gradients
- Purple/blue neon color schemes
- Floating glassmorphism cards
- Excessive shadows
- Empty whitespace without purpose
- Dribbble-style designs that look beautiful but unusable

Avoid all of these.

The design should feel like a product that has gone through multiple years of iteration by a real startup.

Design references:

- Linear
- Notion
- Cursor
- Vercel
- Ramp
- Mercury
- Raycast

Use:

- Tight visual hierarchy
- Purposeful spacing
- Professional typography
- Functional layouts
- Dense but readable information
- Clear navigation
- Consistent component patterns

The interface should prioritize utility over decoration.

Every screen should answer:
"What task is the user trying to complete?"

Avoid decorative elements that do not improve usability.

Animations should be subtle and purposeful.

Cards should feel structured and information-dense rather than oversized and empty.

Use a restrained color system:
- One primary brand color
- Neutral grays
- Minimal accent colors

No rainbow gradients.
No glowing effects.
No glassmorphism.
No futuristic AI aesthetics.

The product should look like software used by hospitals, universities, government organizations, and enterprises.

Think:
"Professional accessibility technology platform"

NOT:
"AI startup landing page template"
